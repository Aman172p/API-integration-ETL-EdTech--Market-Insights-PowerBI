## Problem statement
This project analyzes the YouTube performance of major EdTech players (Xylem, Eduport, and Exam Winner) to identify content trends, viewer behavior, and engagement patterns. By leveraging data from the YouTube API, the analysis provides data-driven recommendations to optimize video duration and content scheduling during exam vs. non-exam periods

## üí°Final-Recommendation

### üéØ Exam Season Strategy
* **Long-Form Dominance:** Prioritize releasing content with durations of **30 minute and 2 hours or more**. Data indicates most students are willing to commit to extended sessions for comprehensive revision during this period.
* **Shorts Optimization:** Increase the frequency of YouTube Shorts to capitalize on high viewership spikes. Focus content on:
    * Exam preparation hacks.
    * Strategies for scoring A+.
    * Motivational content tailored for  students .

### üìÖ Non-Exam Season Strategy
* **Duration Control:** Avoid releasing "Marathon" sessions or videos exceeding **1 to 2 hours**. 
* **Viewer Preference:** Engagement data shows viewers prefer shorter, more concise content during off-peak months; keep videos modular to maintain high retention rates.
